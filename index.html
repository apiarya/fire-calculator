How this calculator works
Phase 1 — Accumulation

While you work, your savings and monthly contributions compound at your pre-retirement return rate. Each year's contributions grow at your raise rate too.

FVsavings = Savings × (1 + r/12)n
FVcontributions = PMT × [(1 + r/12)n − 1] / (r/12)
r = pre-retirement return  ·  n = months to retirement
Phase 2 — How much you'll need

This is the central calculation. Instead of the simple 25× rule, we use the Present Value of a Growing Annuity — a formula that accounts for your exact retirement length, your post-retirement return, and inflation-growing withdrawals.

PMT = your monthly budget, inflated to retirement day
r   = post-retirement monthly return
g   = monthly inflation rate
n   = months in retirement (life expectancy − retire age)
Social Security — two-phase split

If you retire before age 67, Social Security hasn't started yet. The calculator handles this with two segments:

  • Phase A (retire → 67): full inflated budget, no SS offset — PV'd over that window.
  • Phase B (67 → life expectancy): budget minus SS, discounted back to retirement day.

Both phases use the same growing-annuity formula and are combined into a single lump-sum target. This is more accurate than subtracting SS from the monthly budget before multiplying by 25.

Assumptions & limitations
  • Returns are nominal (before inflation) and assumed constant throughout each phase.
  • Social Security is claimed at 67 (full retirement age for those born 1960+).
  • All figures are in today's dollars unless labeled otherwise.
  • No taxes, fees, or sequence-of-returns risk are modeled — treat results as a planning estimate, not a guarantee.
